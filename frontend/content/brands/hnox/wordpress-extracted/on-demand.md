## COLORINGS

[/fusion_text][fusion_separator style_type="single solid" top_margin="0" sep_color="var(--awb-color5)" border_size="1" width="170px" alignment="left" bottom_margin="20" /][fusion_text]

### Giving cold steel, the warmth of traditional hardware.

[/fusion_text][fusion_text]

**Why should we paint stainless when in most cases it is only required to be given "mechanical" treatments (satin-finishing, polishing, etc.)?**

There are three reasons that we consider substantial:

Window hardware has always existed in black, so at the very least, black (RAL 9005) is a color that the market might want and that we can offer.

Increasingly, there is a demand for solutions that dispense with the visual impact of the hardware on the shutter, being able to color it a RAL identical to that used for the window frame serves this purpose.

Prevent the normal yellow patina resulting from the oxidation process of the material, which occurs upon mere contact with air, from occurring. By placing the film of varnish between the material and atmospheric oxygen, oxidation is limited to such a remarkable extent that it is not visible in its most unsightly form, which is the yellow patina that can also be the cause of a window or door no longer being clean and tidy.
